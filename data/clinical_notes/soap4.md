## Clinical Note

**Patient:** 29-year-old Vietnamese female  
**Chief Concern:** Flare of hand eczema

### Subjective
The patient is a 29-year-old Vietnamese female, an established dermatology patient last seen on 07/13/04. She presents today as a referral from ABC, D.O. for reevaluation of hand eczema. Previous treatment has included **Aristocort cream**, **Cetaphil cream**, increased moisturizing lotions, and washing hands with **Cetaphil cleansing lotion**.

She reports a current flare with very dry, cracked hands. She has been washing her hands with soap recently and reports that the Cetaphil cleansing lotion causes burning and pain due to fissures in the skin. She has also been wearing gloves.

The patient is single and currently unemployed.

### Family, Social, and Allergy History
- History of asthma, sinus problems, hives, and psoriasis  
- No known drug allergies  
- Nonsmoker  
- No history of severe sunburns  
- No history of hypertension

### Current Medications
- Claritin (as needed)  
- Zyrtec (as needed)

### Objective

**Physical Examination**
- Bilateral hands are very dry and cracked

### Assessment
- Hand dermatitis

### Plan
1. Discussed treatment plan with the patient and her interpreter.
2. Apply **Aristocort ointment 0.1% mixed with equal parts Polysporin ointment**, three times daily and as needed for itching.
3. Discontinue use of hot soapy water; instead wash hands using **Cetaphil cleansing lotion**.
4. Start **Keflex (cephalexin) 500 mg twice daily for two weeks**, with one refill.
5. Follow up in **one month if symptoms do not improve**, otherwise return as needed.
6. Send a letter to **Dr. XYZ** regarding today’s visit.
