## Clinical Note

**Patient:** 23-year-old White female  
**Chief Complaint:** Allergies

### Subjective
The patient is a 23-year-old White female presenting with complaints of worsening allergy symptoms. She reports a history of allergies while living in Seattle but believes symptoms have become more severe in her current location. She has previously tried antihistamines including **loratadine (Claritin)** and **cetirizine (Zyrtec)**, which initially provided relief but appeared to lose effectiveness over time. She has also used **fexofenadine (Allegra)**, most recently restarting it two weeks ago after using it last summer; however, she reports minimal improvement in symptoms.  

She has used over-the-counter nasal sprays but has not previously used prescription nasal sprays. The patient has a history of **asthma** but does not require daily medication and does not believe her asthma symptoms are currently exacerbated.

### Medications
- Ortho Tri-Cyclen  
- Fexofenadine (Allegra)

### Allergies
- No known drug allergies

### Objective

**Vital Signs**
- Weight: 130 lb  
- Blood Pressure: 124/78 mmHg

**HEENT**
- Throat mildly erythematous without exudate  
- Nasal mucosa erythematous and swollen with clear drainage  
- Tympanic membranes clear bilaterally

**Neck**
- Supple, no lymphadenopathy

**Lungs**
- Clear to auscultation bilaterally

### Assessment
- Allergic rhinitis

### Plan
1. Discontinue **fexofenadine (Allegra)**. Trial **cetirizine (Zyrtec)** again. **Loratadine** may be considered as a lower-cost alternative if needed.
2. Start **mometasone (Nasonex)** nasal spray, two sprays in each nostril daily. Samples provided for three weeks and a prescription was written.
