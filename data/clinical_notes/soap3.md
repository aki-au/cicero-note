## Clinical Note

**Patient:** 62-year-old female  
**Chief Concern:** Dietary consultation for carbohydrate counting in type 1 diabetes

### Subjective
The patient is a 62-year-old female presenting for dietary consultation focused on carbohydrate counting for management of type 1 diabetes. She reports being hospitalized over the weekend for diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA). She states that her blood glucose was 187 mg/dL at bedtime on Friday night and increased to 477 mg/dL upon waking the next morning. She administered a total of 70 additional units of insulin in smaller increments, including 10 units by injection and the remainder through her insulin pump. Despite this, her blood glucose later exceeded 600 mg/dL, prompting hospital evaluation.

She reports that she still does not feel well and currently has symptoms consistent with a cold. She believes this may be related to immune suppression following her recent hospitalization.

### Objective
**Insulin Pump Settings**
- Bolus doses:
  - Breakfast: 5 units
  - Lunch: 6 units
  - Dinner: 11 units
- Basal insulin rate unchanged since last visit with Charla Yassine
- Total basal insulin: 30.5 units per 24 hours

A diet history was obtained during the visit. The patient received instruction on carbohydrate counting with a recommended insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio of **1 unit of insulin for every 10 grams of carbohydrate**. A correction factor of **1 unit of insulin to lower blood glucose by approximately 30 mg/dL** was also recommended.

Educational materials provided and reviewed included the **Lilly guide for meal planning** and an additional **carbohydrate counting reference book**.

### Assessment
The patient was instructed on an insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio of **1 unit per 10 grams of carbohydrates**, consistent with recommendations from a previous visit two years ago, which the patient does not recall. This ratio is based on the **500 rule**.

Education was provided regarding:
- Identification of carbohydrate sources in foods
- Recognition of **15-gram carbohydrate equivalents**
- Importance of administering insulin at the time carbohydrates are consumed

The patient appears to have a pattern of correcting elevated blood glucose levels later in the day rather than adjusting carbohydrate intake or insulin dosing earlier.

An alternative strategy discussed was maintaining consistent carbohydrate intake at meals with corresponding consistent insulin dosing. Based on this approach, recommended carbohydrate intake includes:
- Breakfast: **45 g carbohydrates (3 servings)**
- Lunch: **45 g carbohydrates (3 servings)**
- Dinner: **60 g carbohydrates (4 servings)**

Joanne Araiza briefly joined the consultation to evaluate whether the patient's insulin pump was functioning appropriately. The patient was provided with the manufacturer’s support number to contact if there are concerns about pump malfunction or insulin delivery issues.

### Plan
1. Use an **insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio of 1 unit of insulin per 10 grams of carbohydrate consumed**.
2. Utilize carbohydrate counting to guide insulin dosing or alternatively maintain **consistent carbohydrate intake at meals**.
3. Follow recommended carbohydrate targets:
   - Breakfast: 45 g
   - Lunch: 45 g
   - Dinner: 60 g
4. Monitor insulin pump function and contact manufacturer support if delivery concerns arise.
5. One-hour dietary consultation completed. Patient was provided contact information for follow-up if additional questions or needs arise.
